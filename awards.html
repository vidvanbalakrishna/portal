Awards & Honors

Celebrating excellence and dedication to preserving the Mysore tradition

2025

Nada Vallabha

Nada Sudha, Chennai

Conferred during their twenty-seventh annual music and dance festival in recognition of outstanding contribution to Carnatic music.

2024

Picchumanui Iyer Centenary Award

Parthasarathi Swami Sabha, Chennai

Prestigious award honoring the legacy of Picchumanui Iyer, presented in recognition of exceptional artistic achievement.

2023

Central Sangeet Natak Akademi Award

Government of India

Presented by the Hon. President of India at Vigyan Bhawan, New Delhi. India's highest recognition for performing artists, honoring outstanding contribution to Indian music and the Mysore Veena tradition.

2022

Sangeeta Kala Ratna

Bangalore Gayana Samaja

Prestigious honor from one of India's premier music organizations, recognizing lifetime contribution to Carnatic music excellence.

2019

Veena Nadamani

Charubala Trust, Chennai

Title conferred by Violin Maestro M. Chandrashekaran under the auspices of the Charubala Trust, recognizing mastery of the veena.

2018

Parampara Pradheepanaacharya & Lifetime Achievement

Chintalapalli Parampara Trust

Dual honor recognizing both teaching excellence and lifetime artistic achievements in preserving the Mysore Veena parampara. The trust carries 800 years of rich history.

2016

Veena Sheshanna Memorial National Award

Swaramurthy V.N. Rao Trust

National award honoring the legacy of the legendary Veena Sheshanna, recognizing authentic preservation of the Mysore school tradition.

2014

Karnataka Kalashree

Karnataka Sangeetha Nrithya Academy

State's highest honor for performing artists, recognizing exceptional contribution to Karnataka's cultural heritage and excellence in preserving the Mysore style and technique of veena.

2013

Vainika Brahma

Naada Brahma Sangeetha Sabha, Mysore

Prestigious title conferred in recognition of supreme mastery of the veena and dedication to the art form.

2009

Asthana Vidvan

Sri Kamakoti Peetham, Kanchi

Appointed as court musician to one of India's most revered religious institutions, continuing the tradition of royal patronage of classical arts.

2003

Best Lecture Demonstration of Pallavis

Karnataka Gana Kala Parishat

Recognized for exceptional educational presentation at the annual music conference, demonstrating deep knowledge and teaching excellence.

1997

Ganakalashri

Karnataka Gana Kala Parishat

Title conferred in recognition of outstanding musical artistry and contribution to Carnatic music in Karnataka.

1979 to 2008

Best Veena Player (5 Times)

Madras Music Academy

Honored as Best Veena Player five times (1979, 1991, 1996, 2006, 2008) by the prestigious Madras Music Academy, testament to consistent excellence over three decades.
